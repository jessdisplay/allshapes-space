Stage 1 · Months 1–3 · trial
$20k rent + $10k launch

Flexible and obligation-free: the trial exists so both sides can test the working relationship and the operating model. Either side can step away at the end of the quarter with nothing further owed beyond the quarter's agreed contributions. Jesse's half-share for the quarter is ≈ $30,000. $20,000 is paid as rent — one month in advance, on the 1st of each month. Rent is inclusive of the robotics: Wonder Byte programs the robots for All Shapes events and experiences as part of the rent — no separate engineering or programming charges. The remaining ≈ $10,000 is not paid as rent: by agreement it is redirected into the opening-night budget — catering & food, photographers & videographers, flying in special guests — spent through the shared ledger. Same total contribution; the rent shortfall is made up as the launch investment that starts the space earning. Weekly walk-throughs to launch; joint calendar; decisions by the two of us only.

Stage 2 · Months 4–6
50 / 50

A structured operating partnership with transparent costs and profit sharing: full equal partnership on rent and the venue P&L — rent paid one month in advance on the 1st; costs, event profits, coworking and F&B income split evenly through the same open ledger. Robot programming for events stays included in the rent — no separate charges.

Stage 3 · Month 7 onward
Co-founders

A formal long-term venture: 50/50 venture entity for All Shapes Club — roles, brand and IP ownership, profit distribution, asset ownership, capital recognition and possible expansion — papered with proper advice before entering.

1Gino's fitout — acknowledged from day one

Wonder Byte's existing investment of ≈ $500,000 in the fitout, robotics and equipment is formally acknowledged from the beginning of this agreement and recorded at an agreed value. The final asset recovery / ownership model is not decided now — both sides agree it will be properly addressed, with accountants, before entering the Stage 3 long-term venture. structure with accountants

2First right on the lease

Purely a "just in case", for later: should Gino ever choose to break the lease or sell his interest in the space, Jesse / All Shapes holds first right to take over the lease and/or acquire that interest at fair value before any third party. No obligation on either side — it simply protects what we build together.

  • The network: UQ as key partner & sponsor, UniQuest Extension Fund, investor interest incl. Michael Batko (Startmate), keen customers

4In-kind collaboration & first right of work

Beyond the venue, the partnership extends to each other's projects and customers. Kin Ventures leads — and is offered first — all branding, UX/UI, websites, architecture and design work across joint projects; Wonder Byte leads all technical, AI and robotics work. Both sides may contribute work in kind, recorded through the shared ledger at agreed rates — for example, Wonder Byte acting as the technical team on Kin Ventures projects (such as Rise), or Kin Ventures delivering branding, design and fitout services to Wonder Byte customers who need them. Wonder Byte and Kin Ventures remain separate businesses: each company separately scopes, quotes and invoices its own customer projects, unless a project is specifically agreed by both sides as a joint All Shapes project — those run through the shared ledger. Referrals flow both ways.

5How the space earns

  • Events: hire + robot experiences + F&B (draft rate card live on the site)
  • Coworking: free hot desks downstairs (paid coffee/food) · $500/mo dedicated mezzanine desks
  • House cellar poured at events — stock at cost to Gino, margin to the venture
  • Venue income first covers approved venue operating costs — transparent records through the open ledger, major expenses jointly approved by both partners
  • What remains: event & coworking profits credit rent 50/50 until the space pays for itself
