The experiences

Service, performed by robots

Every experience is a programmed, rehearsed service module — mix and match per event.

01 · arrival

The robot welcome

Cruzr greets your guests by voice at the heritage entry, explains the room, and escorts anyone who wants guiding — multilingual, no touch required.

02 · service

The canapé circuit

Cadebot circulates trays on a programmed loop — announcing what's on board, waiting while guests choose, never blocking a path.

03 · coffee

The robot barista

Order by voice, watch the arms work, hear your name at hand-off. The coffee bar runs live through your whole event.

04 · showpiece

The humanoid moment

Unitree G1 performs on a schedule in its own corner — the photo everyone leaves with, timed and announced so nobody misses it.

The difference

Programmed to include every body

Voice-first everything

Greeting, ordering, guiding: nothing requires seeing or touching a screen.

Wheelchair-first trays

Announced tray heights, offset stop positions, generous dwell times.

Say it, show it, light it

Every announcement is voiced, displayed large-print, and lit — deaf and blind guests get everything.

A quiet zone

One robot-free, low-sensory corner at every event, told to every guest on arrival.

Rehearsed with lived experience

Demos tested with wheelchair users and blind testers before your night.

Step-free heritage entry

Ground-level access from St Pauls Terrace, accessible circulation ≥1800 mm at every event layout.

The team

The people behind the space

Three decades of hospitality, a builder's eye, and inclusive design — the Hub is run by people who have done this before.

Founder · creative & inclusion

Jesse Costelloe

Founder of All Shapes. Designer and technologist working at the intersection of accessibility, hospitality and emerging technology. Programs the guest experience: every robot script, route and accessible service pattern in the space.

Partner · construction & fitouts

Greg Buckley

Runs a construction and fitout organisation. Leads the build side of the space — staging, accessibility works, and keeping a heritage shell working as a modern venue. If it needs measuring, moving or making, it's Greg's.

Partner · hospitality & venues

Mick Fallon

Thirty years in hospitality, managing some of Brisbane's most iconic venues. Leads venue operations, food and beverage, and event delivery — the reason the robots are backed by service that would stand up without them.

With Gino Feng and the Wonder Byte Technology team providing the fleet, the fitout, and the robotics expertise on site.
